This paper proposes treating coordination as a configurable architectural layer in multi-agent systems to improve their performance and reliability. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach using an information-controlled design on prediction markets and report promising results. Optimal Trading in Automated Market Makers with Deep Learning(https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.02180)

This article explores the optimization of trading strategies in Constant Function Market Makers and centralized exchanges using deep learning. The authors develop a model that accounts for the interaction between these two markets and pose an optimal execution problem where the agent hides their orders by controlling the rate at which they trade. The results show that the optimal strategy is not prone to price slippage and outperforms naive strategies.

Trade Execution Flow as the Underlying Source of Market Dynamics(https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.01471)

This study demonstrates that trade execution flow is the fundamental driving force of market dynamics. The authors develop a numerical framework to calculate execution flow from the data using the Radon-Nikodym derivative and introduce a framework based on the Christoffel function spectrum. The results show that the technique can automatically determine thresholds that can serve as actionable triggers and determine the characteristic time scale directly from the corresponding eigenproblem.

Single-Period Portfolio Selection via Information Projection(https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03184)

This paper presents a novel approach to single-period portfolio selection under CRRA utility, using information-theoretic concepts such as R\'enyi divergence and entropy. The key finding is that CRRA portfolio selection can be reformulated as a R\'enyi information-projection problem, which can be solved using an alternating optimization algorithm. This method has been shown to require fewer iterations than existing methods in the low risk-aversion regime, making it a potentially more efficient approach to portfolio selection.
