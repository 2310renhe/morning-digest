Dynamic Collateral Control for Permissionless Spot Perpetual Basis Trading (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05089)

This paper introduces a dynamic collateral control strategy for permissionless spot-perpetual basis trading in decentralized finance. The strategy aims to optimize the allocation of capital between spot inventory and derivative margin, taking into account on-chain liquidity and execution frictions. The research provides three key results, including a static control problem solution, an asymmetric dynamic extension, and an execution-aware implementation with live routed execution and historical backtests, which can help improve the efficiency and robustness of basis trading in decentralized finance.

What Can Go Wrong During Caplet Stripping ? (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05140)

This study examines the challenges of extracting caplet volatilities from market cap quotes and identifies interpolation schemes and node placement as primary drivers of instability. The authors propose practical remedies, including continuous flat-linear and C1 flat-smooth kernels, to preserve bootstrap equivalence and ensure positivity. Numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of these remedies in reducing oscillations and delivering a stable caplet stripping workflow.

Fast and General Simulation of L\'evy-driven Ornstein Uhlenbeck processes for Energy Derivatives (https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15483)

This paper introduces a new simulation technique for Lévy-driven Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes, which is designed to be general, fast, and accurate. The technique relies on the numerical inversion of the characteristic function and leverages FFT to ensure efficient simulations. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this methodology in pricing energy derivatives, showing that it is at least one order of magnitude faster than existing algorithms while maintaining equivalent accuracy.
