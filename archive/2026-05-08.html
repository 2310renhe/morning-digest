Details:

A Review of Large Language Models for Stock Price Forecasting from a Hedge-Fund Perspective (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05211)

This review synthesizes applications of large language models in stock price forecasting, including sentiment analysis and multi-agent trading systems. It highlights practical pitfalls such as fragility in sentiment analysis and dataset design. The review aims to guide researchers and hedge fund managers in integrating large language models into trading pipelines.

Probabilistic Predictions of Option Prices with Modular Approximate Bayesian Inference (https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.00658)

This paper proposes a new approximate Bayesian inferential framework for probabilistic predictions of option prices, exploiting multiple information sources. The approach operates directly from the theoretical option pricing model and does not require an explicit statistical model. It demonstrates accurate probabilistic option-price predictions in realistic scenarios.

CatNet: Controlling the False Discovery Rate in LSTM with SHAP Feature Importance and Gaussian Mirrors (https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.16666)

CatNet is an algorithm that controls the False Discovery Rate and selects significant features in LSTM, employing SHAP values and the Gaussian Mirror algorithm. It proposes a new kernel-based independence measure to avoid instability due to nonlinear correlations among features. CatNet performs robustly on different model settings with both simulated and real-world data.

Numerical methods for lambda quantiles: robust evaluation and portfolio optimisation (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06220)

This paper presents efficient algorithms for computing lambda quantiles and demonstrates their application in portfolio optimisation. The algorithm, {\Lambda}-Newton-Bis, combines Newton's method with a bisection strategy to ensure global convergence. The algorithm handles potential discontinuities and achieves local quadratic convergence under standard regularity assumptions.

A Geometry-Aware Residual Correction of Hagan's SABR Implied Volatility Formula (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06604)

This paper proposes a hybrid methodology to improve the approximation of SABR implied volatility by combining analytical structure with machine learning. The approach augments the neural-network input representation with geometric features derived from the stochastic differential equations of the SABR model. The resulting model acts as a structured residual correction to the analytical formula, retaining interpretability while capturing higher-order effects.

Frustrated Dynamics of Distance Matrices (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05376)

This paper introduces the Frustrated Distance Matrix (FDM) model, a dynamic extension of the static distance-matrix ensemble. The time-resolved FDM spectrum carries information about structural changes of the underlying point process. The paper finds that the static BBS template is preserved at every time, with the dynamics entering as a redistribution of spectral mass within that template.

Multi-Dimensional Behavioral Evaluation of Agentic Stock Prediction Systems Using LLM Judges with Closed-Loop Reinforcement Learning Feedback (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05739)

This paper presents a behavioral evaluation framework that addresses the gap in evaluating agentic stock prediction systems. The framework uses an ensemble of three large language model (LLM) judges to score behavioral traces along six domain-specific dimensions. The framework is used to fine-tune the system, resulting in a one-day MAPE reduction and an 18% Sharpe ratio improvement.

INEUS: Iterative Neural Solver for High-Dimensional PIDEs (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06281)

This paper introduces INEUS, a meshfree iterative neural solver for partial integro-differential equations (PIDEs). The method replaces the explicit evaluation of nonlocal jump integrals with single-jump sampling and reformulates PIDE solving as a sequence of recursive regression problems. INEUS delivers accurate and scalable solutions for various high-dimensional linear and nonlinear examples.

SNAPO: Smooth Neural Adjoint Policy Optimization for Optimal Control via Differentiable Simulation (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06570)

This paper introduces SNAPO, a framework that embeds a neural policy inside a known, differentiable simulator. SNAPO computes exact gradients of the objective with respect to all policy parameters and all inputs in a single adjoint pass. The framework is demonstrated on three domains, including natural gas storage, pension fund asset-liability management, and pharmaceutical manufacturing.

Proactive Market Making and Liquidity Analysis for Everlasting Options in DeFi Ecosystems (https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.07068)

This paper offers an in-depth analysis of markets for everlasting options, modeled using a dynamic proactive market maker. The paper examines the behavior of funding fees and transaction costs across varying liquidity conditions. The results demonstrate that liquidity providers can achieve a net positive PnL by employing effective hedging strategies.

Analytic approximation for Bachelier option prices and applications (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02040)

This paper writes the price for out-of-the-money (OTM) and in-the-money (ITM) options as an expansion with respect to the moneyness, where the coefficients are related to the negative powers of the future mean volatility. The paper uses this expansion as a control variate to reduce the variance of Monte Carlo option prices in the correlated case.

What Can Go Wrong During Caplet Stripping ? (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05140)

This paper studies exact and near exact extraction of caplet volatilities from market cap quotes and identifies why some common choices produce extreme oscillations or negative vols. The paper proposes practical remedies, including continuous flat-linear and C1 flat-smooth kernels, midpoint node placement, and positivity enforcement via an exponential reparametrization.

On Utility Maximization under Multivariate Fake Stationary Affine Volterra Models (https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11046)

This paper is concerned with Merton's portfolio optimization problem in a Volterra stochastic environment described by a multivariate fake stationary Volterra--Heston model. The paper tackles the problem using a stochastic factor solution to a Riccati backward stochastic differential equation (BSDE). The resulting optimal strategies for Merton's problems are derived in semi-closed form depending on the solutions to time-dependent multivariate Riccati-Volterra equations.
