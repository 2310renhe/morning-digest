A Review of Large Language Models for Stock Price Forecasting from a Hedge-Fund Perspective

This review synthesizes recent applications of large language models in stock price forecasting, highlighting practical pitfalls such as fragility in sentiment analysis and data leakage. The review guides academic researchers and hedge fund managers in integrating large language models into real-world trading pipelines. By addressing these challenges, the review aims to improve the robustness of large language models in stock price forecasting.

Probabilistic Predictions of Option Prices with Modular Approximate Bayesian Inference

This framework proposes a new approach to calculating probabilistic predictions of future option prices, leveraging multiple information sources and operating directly from theoretical option pricing models. The approach demonstrates accurate probabilistic option-price predictions in realistic scenarios and is robust to option-pricing errors. The method's rapidity of real-time updates of predictive distributions is also highlighted, making it suitable for practical applications.

CatNet: Controlling the False Discovery Rate in LSTM with SHAP Feature Importance and Gaussian Mirrors

CatNet is an algorithm that effectively controls the false discovery rate and selects significant features in LSTM models, employing SHAP values to quantify feature importance and constructing a mirror statistic for FDR control. The algorithm performs robustly on different model settings with both simulated and real-world data, reducing overfitting and improving model interpretability. CatNet's framework can be extended to other time-series or sequential deep learning models, making it a versatile tool for improving model robustness.

Numerical methods for lambda quantiles (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06220)

The paper introduces efficient algorithms for computing lambda quantiles, a generalisation of classical value at risk. The proposed algorithm, Λ-Newton-Bis, combines Newton's method with a bisection strategy to ensure global convergence. The method is applied in portfolio optimisation, demonstrating computational efficiency and practical relevance.

A Geometry-Aware Residual Correction of Hagan's SABR Implied Volatility Formula (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06604)

This paper proposes a hybrid methodology to improve the approximation of SABR implied volatility. The approach combines analytical structure with machine learning, preserving the analytical backbone while capturing higher-order effects. The resulting model acts as a structured residual correction to the analytical formula, retaining interpretability while improving accuracy and robustness.

Frustrated Dynamics of Distance Matrices (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05376)

The Frustrated Distance Matrix model is introduced, analysing the time-resolved spectrum of distance matrices to understand structural changes in the underlying point process. The model is applied to a system of Brownian particles on a sphere, demonstrating the ability to flag ring formation and extract spectral diagnostics of the structural change.

Multi-Dimensional Behavioral Evaluation of Agentic Stock Prediction Systems (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05739)

This paper presents a framework for behavioural evaluation of agentic stock prediction systems. The framework uses large language model judges and closed-loop reinforcement learning feedback to improve decision-making quality. The approach is demonstrated on a system that makes sequences of interdependent decisions, showing improved performance and correlation with realized Sharpe ratio.

INEUS: Iterative Neural Solver for High-Dimensional PIDEs (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06281)

INEUS is a meshfree iterative neural solver for partial integro-differential equations. The method replaces explicit evaluation of nonlocal jump integrals with single-jump sampling and reformulates PIDE solving as a sequence of recursive regression problems. INEUS is shown to deliver accurate and scalable solutions for various high-dimensional linear and nonlinear examples.

SNAPO: Smooth Neural Adjoint Policy Optimization (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06570)

SNAPO embeds a neural policy inside a known, differentiable simulator, computing exact gradients of the objective with respect to all policy parameters and inputs in a single adjoint pass. The approach is demonstrated on three domains, including natural gas storage, pension fund asset-liability management, and pharmaceutical manufacturing, showing improved performance and sensitivity analysis.

Proactive Market Making and Liquidity Analysis for Everlasting Options (https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.07068)

This paper analyses markets for everlasting options using a dynamic proactive market maker. The approach examines funding fees and transaction costs, providing insights into effective hedging strategies and liquidity provider incentives. The results demonstrate that liquidity providers can achieve a net positive PnL by employing effective hedging strategies.

Analytic approximation for Bachelier option prices (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02040)

The paper derives an analytic approximation for Bachelier option prices via classical Itô calculus and Taylor expansions. The approximation is used as a control variate to reduce the variance of Monte Carlo option prices in the correlated case.

What Can Go Wrong During Caplet Stripping (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05140)

This paper identifies common pitfalls in caplet stripping, such as extreme oscillations or negative vols. Practical remedies are proposed, including continuous flat-linear and C1 flat-smooth kernels and positivity enforcement. The approach is demonstrated on numerical experiments, showing substantially reduced oscillations and robust positive caplet curves.

On Utility Maximization under Multivariate Fake Stationary Affine Volterra Models (https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11046)

The paper tackles Merton's portfolio optimization problem in a non-Markovian and non-semimartingale Volterra stochastic environment. The approach uses a stochastic factor solution to a Riccati backward stochastic differential equation, deriving optimal strategies in semi-closed form depending on solutions to Riccati-Volterra equations.
