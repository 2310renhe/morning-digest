Imperfect Commitment in Maximal Extractable Value Auctions(https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22667)

  • The research question is to study the commitment problem in MEV auctions, where Ethereum block builders may defect from auction outcomes with a probability of $\varepsilon$.
  • The method involves modeling a builder who defects with probability $\varepsilon$ and replicates a type-specific fraction $\gamma(\tau)$ of the winning MEV opportunity, and using the \texttt{libmev} dataset to estimate $\gamma(\tau)$.
  • Key findings include:

- The cost of imperfect commitment depends jointly on replicability and competition.

- The resulting equilibrium is piecewise.

- There is sharp heterogeneity across MEV types, with sandwich opportunities being highly competitive, while naked arbitrage and liquidations leave substantially more surplus exposed to builder defection.

Faster Monotone Implied Volatility Solver (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22427)

  • The paper presents ThiopheneIV, a Black-Scholes implied-volatility solver with a monotone core and explicit production guards.
  • ThiopheneIV is compared to a Java port of J\"ackel's Let's Be Rational, and is found to be faster while keeping regular-grid errors close.
  • The solver starts from the non-iterative Choi-Huh-Su L3 lower-bound seed and applies three Euler-Chebyshev corrections.
  • The implementation includes parity normalisation, microscopic Bachelier-limit handling, saturated-price treatment, finite-update checks, fallback seeds, and an optional J\"ackel-Newton polish.

From Arbitrage Removal to Density Extraction: A Model-Free Framework for Short-Dated Options (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22792)

  • The paper develops a model-free pipeline for risk-neutral density extraction from short-dated option chains, consisting of two steps: arbitrage removal and smooth entropic density extraction.
  • The pipeline is tested on synthetic Heston panels and short-dated SPX option data, sampled from a few hours to one week before expiry.
  • The pipeline returns robust densities across various market conditions, including scheduled macroeconomic announcements.
  • The recovered densities are used to construct short-dated implied-volatility smiles.

What Does Deep Hedging Actually Learn? Delta Corrections, Regime Fragility, and Symbolic Distillation (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21696)

  • The paper studies empirical deep hedging for S&P 500 index options under a local downside-shortfall reward.
  • TD3 agents are compared with a daily-updated Black-Scholes delta hedge on the same option episodes.
  • The agents learn a systematic delta haircut relative to Black-Scholes, which is explained by spot-implied-volatility co-movement.
  • The correction improves accumulated reward and terminal downside variance, but is regime-fragile, with losses in adverse daily states in 2022 and underhedging raising ordinary variance in 2023.
  • Symbolic regression distills the neural policies into compact formulas that preserve much of the reward, downside-variance, and CVaR advantage over Black-Scholes.

A Generative Adversarial Graph Neural Network for Synthetic Time Series Data (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22215)

  • The paper introduces the Sig-Graph GAN model, which integrates the time-series signature, Long Short-Term Memory network, and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to generate synthetic time series data.
  • The model uses the visibility graph algorithm to derive a graph-based representation of the underlying time series.
  • Numerical evaluations demonstrate that the Sig-Graph GAN model outperforms baseline methods in replicating the distribution of logarithmic returns across different stock exchanges.
  • The integration of the graph structure with the autoregressive component effectively captures both geometric and temporal patterns embedded in time-series data.
