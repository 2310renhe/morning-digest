FinStressTS: A Parametric Synthetic Benchmark for Time-Series Forecasting in Finance (https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03184)

  • The paper introduces FinStressTS, a mechanism-aware synthetic benchmark that links model behavior to controlled structural causes.
  • FinStressTS comprises 30 diagnostic environments around six mechanism families: volatility clustering, multi-scale persistence, heavy-tailed shocks, regime switching, self-exciting jumps, and zero-inflated processes.
  • 15 models are benchmarked, including classical methods (HAR, VAR) and deep probabilistic architectures (DeepAR, TSFlow).
  • Performance is mechanism-dependent, with autoregressive and linear models competitive in several environments.
  • Distributional alignment matters, with parametric probabilistic models calibrating well in stationary settings.

Regime-Arrival Uncertainty in Generalization Bounds under Distribution Shift (https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02657)

  • The paper proposes a framework that generalizes regime-aware models by quantifying the extra risk due to regime composition mismatch.
  • The framework is applied to 25 years of global equity indices.
  • The proposed penalty is an ex post realized generalization gap.

Hybrid News Sentiment Engine: Real-Time Market Analysis via Adaptive Ensemble Learning on News-Price Pairs (https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03457)

  • The paper presents a hybrid news sentiment engine that combines a financial-domain lexicon, an adaptive statistical TF-IDF cluster learner, and an auto-calibrating weighting mechanism.
  • The engine uses the Tradeflags NewsFeed API, which provides 22 price-snapshot fields per news item for equity indices (ES, NQ, SPY, DJIA, NDX, IWM), commodities (CL), and cryptocurrencies (BTC, ETH).
  • The engine is compared to established methods, including FinBERT, GPT-based scoring, VADER, and commercial sentiment APIs.

Market Simulation under Adverse Selection (https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.12721)

  • The paper studies the effects of fill probabilities and adverse fills on trading strategy simulation.
  • The study focuses on a stochastic optimal control market-making problem and tests the strategy on ES, NQ, CL, and ZN futures contracts listed on the CME.
  • The paper provides empirical evidence that fill probabilities and adverse fills can significantly affect performance.

A Practical Guide to Strip Caplet Volatilities (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05140)

  • The paper presents a practical workflow for recovering caplet volatility term structure consistent with quoted cap volatilities.
  • The workflow includes criteria on input data, robust outlier detection, and a direct non-bootstrap stripping approach.
  • The paper introduces compact-kernel transition interpolants and global search methods for a richer, smoother curve.

Explicit Rational Formulae for Bachelier (Normal) Implied Volatility (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18343)

  • The paper presents two explicit rational formulae for Bachelier implied volatility.
  • The formulae take option price, forward, strike, and expiry as inputs and return the implied normal volatility without iteration.
  • The formulae follow the branch structure of LFK-4, but use a simpler near-the-money variable.

AlphaEval: A Comprehensive and Efficient Evaluation Framework for Formula Alpha Mining (https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.13174)

  • The paper proposes AlphaEval, a unified, parallelizable, and backtest-free evaluation framework for automated alpha mining models.
  • AlphaEval assesses the overall quality of generated alphas along five dimensions: predictive power, stability, robustness, financial logic, and diversity.
  • The framework is open-sourced to promote reproducibility and community engagement.
