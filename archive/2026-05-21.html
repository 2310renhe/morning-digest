Continuous Timing Signals for Growth-Defensive Style Allocation (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20636)

  • The paper studies conditional allocation between a growth/technology ETF basket ($G$) and a defensive income/value-oriented ETF basket ($D$) using macro-market timing signals.
  • The allocation framework combines rate relief, SPY drawdown depth, high-VIX stress relief, and a growth-crowding penalty to generate a continuous smooth score.
  • The smooth-score policy uses a 50% maximum active tilt and obtains a 19.24% CAGR, a Sharpe ratio of 1.01, and a maximum drawdown of -31.63% from June 28, 2017 to May 15, 2026.
  • The selected policy improves over 50/50 G/D, matched TNX-only, matched core-only, SPY, and volatility-matched 100% G benchmarks.
  • Companies mentioned: None
  • Quantitative figures: 0.273 market beta, -0.552 HML beta, 0.117 momentum beta, 1.95% annualized alpha, 0.81 Newey-West t-statistic, 19.24% CAGR, 1.01 Sharpe ratio, -31.63% maximum drawdown, 50% maximum active tilt, 10bp transaction costs

  • The paper models portfolio crossing as limited-communication preference elicitation over signed portfolio trades in institutional crossing markets.
  • The analysis uses price-directed demand queries and verification queries to search the portfolio space and guide queries.
  • Market-calibrated experiments using equity panels from the United States, Korea, Japan, and Germany show that the hybrid procedure recovers 88% of full-information welfare under a limited query budget.
  • Companies mentioned: None
