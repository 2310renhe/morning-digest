The Payment Heterogeneity Index (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12547)

  • The Payment Heterogeneity Index (PHI) is a composite statistic for overseeing post-award payments in public procurement.
  • PHI combines a tail-behaviour component and a structural-dispersion component, computed via Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) estimation.
  • Applied to UK municipal procurement data, PHI identifies 10.1% of high-volume suppliers with structurally significant payment patterns.
  • The Coefficient of Variation (ρ=0.310) is compared, with PHI offering unique insights into regime separation.

Enhancing a Risk Model by Adding Transient Statistical Factors (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12977)

  • The proposed method enhances existing factor models by refining them and adding new statistical factors based on maximum likelihood estimation.
  • The approach is demonstrated on the Barra short-term US risk model for a universe of US high-capitalization equities.
  • The method applies to situations with missing asset returns, making it suitable for typical equity datasets.
  • Two hyperparameters are used: the number of additional factors and the half-life parameter for weighting returns in the log-likelihood objective.

Vector-Quantized Discrete Latent Factors Meet Financial Priors (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13407)

  • PRISM-VQ is a dynamic factor framework that integrates expert prior factors, vector-quantized discrete latent factors, and a structure-conditioned Mixture-of-Experts.
  • Vector quantization captures robust market structure, while discrete codes serve as latent factors and routing signals for temporal expert specialization.
  • Experiments on CSI 300 and S&P 500 show improvements in cross-sectional return prediction and portfolio performance over strong baselines.
  • The code for PRISM-VQ is available at https://github.com/finxlab/PRISM-VQ.

Multi-Dimensional Behavioral Evaluation of Agentic Stock Prediction Systems Using Large Language Model Judges with Closed-Loop Reinforcement Learning Feedback (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05739)

  • The research question revolves around evaluating the process of forecast generation in agentic stock prediction systems.
  • The methodology uses an ensemble of three large language model (LLM) judges to score behavioral traces along six dimensions: regime detection, routing, adaptation, risk calibration, strategy coherence, and error recovery.
  • The dimensions are scored using a perturbation procedure, with cross-model agreement reaching Krippendorff's α = 0.85.
  • The composite behavioral score correlates with the realized 20-day Sharpe ratio at Spearman ρ = 0.72.
  • The framework uses a Soft Actor-Critic reward with a credit-assigned penalty based on deficient per-dimension scores.
  • Three fine-tuning cycles produce a one-day MAPE reduction from 0.61% to 0.54% (11.5% relative, p < 0.001, Cohen's d = 0.31) in the held-out 2017-2025 test period.
  • The results are significant under a Diebold-Mariano test of equal predictive accuracy (DM = -7.83) and localized by a Giacomini-White conditional predictive ability test to the high-volatility regime.
  • No specific companies are mentioned in the study.
  • The methodology is application-agnostic, and results are from offline backtesting, not addressing effects specific to live deployment.
