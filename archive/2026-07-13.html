Herding and Liquidity in Order-Book Markets. I. A Robust Liquidity-Stress Crossover and its Reflexive Mechanism (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08907)

  • The study applies Bouchaud's phase-diagram method to a continuous-double-auction order-book model, mapping the full phase diagram and testing its robustness.
  • The model includes fundamental-anchored zero-intelligence liquidity and a mid-anchored chartist herding layer, controlled by the fraction φ and the strength κ of herders.
  • A 7x6 grid (336 runs) locates an emergent liquidity-stress crossover, with the order parameter rising to about 0.34 at (φ,κ)=(0.9,1.0).
  • The crossover is rule-robust, horizon-robust, and has a monotone onset boundary φ*(κ) = {0.55, 0.45, 0.36}.

When Does Order Flow Matter? State-Dependent L2 Liquidity-State Transitions in Crypto Futures (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09230)
