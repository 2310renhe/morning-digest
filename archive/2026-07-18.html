How Much of a 10-K Matters? Aggregation-Dependent Value of Full-Text versus Risk-Factor Sentiment (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14174)

  • The study extends a supervised lexicon-learning approach to 10-K filings and their Item 1A risk-factor sections.
  • 1,383 filings from 94 Nasdaq-100 technology constituents (2006-2023) are analyzed, with sentiment scores trained against return and volatility labels at three levels of aggregation: sector, portfolio, and individual firm.
  • Full-filing text produces more accurate sentiment at the sector and portfolio level, but the narrower Item 1A section performs better at the individual-firm level.
  • A Loughran-McDonald dictionary baseline is consistently negatively correlated with price at every level tested.

Thermodynamic theory of voting and EU elections (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15119)

  • The theory traces parallels between system energies of coupled nonlinear oscillators and party vote fractions, describing the distribution of party votes in EU elections.
  • The Rayleigh-Jeans (RJ) thermalization and condensation at low energy states are used to describe the wealth inequality in society.
  • The RJ theory depicts the Lorenz and Pareto curves obtained from the EU vote results and recovers the dispersion of votes between candidates of first-round presidential elections in France.

When Directional Accuracy Lies: A Base-Rate-Honest Benchmark for LoRA-Adapted TimesFM on Equity Forecasting (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12248)

  • The study evaluates the directional accuracy of LoRA-adapted TimesFM on equity forecasting, using a benchmark with expanding walk-forward folds and honest baselines (zero-shot TimesFM, always-up, random-walk, persistence, AR(1)).
  • The benchmark is applied to two universes: a tech-heavy NASDAQ-100 and a broad S&P 500.
  • Results show that fine-tuning TimesFM does not beat naive baselines and confers no tradeable directional edge, but reduces point-forecast error relative to zero-shot TimesFM.

NeuralChaos: Optimal Adapted Approximation of Square Integrable Predictable Processes (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14361)

  • The paper addresses challenges in representing and computing $\mathbb{R}^{d}$-valued predictable square-integrable processes over $[0,T]$.
  • NeuralChaos is introduced as a neural operator architecture that produces elements of $\mathcal{H}^2_T(\mathbb{R}^{d})$ using only finitely many evaluations of the driving Brownian motion.
  • NeuralChaos is proven to be dense in $\mathcal{H}^2_T(\mathbb{R}^{d})$ and achieves the best $N$-term chaoslet approximation rates for compressible and Malliavin--Sobolev regular processes.
  • No specific companies are mentioned in the paper.
  • Numerical experiments on a stochastic optimal control problem and dynamic hedging demonstrate the practical effectiveness of NeuralChaos.

Structure-Aware Variational State Preparation for Quantum Basket Option Pricing (https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14518)

  • The paper proposes a structure-aware quantum state-preparation framework for quantum amplitude estimation (QAE)-based basket option pricing.
  • The framework uses tensor-train (TT) rank information to design shallow variational state-preparation circuits.
  • In the independent regime, TT ranks remove unnecessary entangling links from a hardware-efficient ansatz.
  • In correlated basket settings, asset-wise marginals are prepared locally and a compact latent block is trained to match the basket cumulative distribution function.
  • No specific companies are mentioned in the paper.
  • Numerical experiments show that the proposed circuits replace the exponential state-preparation depth scaling of exact amplitude loading with linear scaling, while maintaining low-percent basket-pricing errors.
