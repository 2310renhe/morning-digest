microgpt (http://karpathy.github.io/2026/02/12/microgpt/)

Extract ALL key points from the source. Cover every major argument, data point, and conclusion. Use bullet points for clarity:

* The microgpt project is a 200-line Python script that trains and infers a GPT model with no dependencies.

* The script is a culmination of multiple projects (micrograd, makemore, nanogpt, etc.) and a decade-long obsession to simplify LLMs to their bare essentials.

* The script contains the full algorithmic content of what is needed: dataset of documents, tokenizer, autograd engine, a GPT-2-like neural network architecture, the Adam optimizer, training loop, and inference loop.

* The dataset used is a list of 32,000 names, one per line, which is used to train the model to generate new, plausible-sounding names.

* The tokenizer is a simple character-level tokenizer that assigns a unique integer to each character in the dataset.

* The autograd engine is implemented from scratch using a single class called `Value`, which wraps a scalar number and tracks how it was computed.

* The `Value` class has a `backward` method that walks the computation graph in reverse topological order, applying the chain rule at each step.

* The model architecture is a stateless function that takes a token, a position, the parameters, and the cached keys/values from previous positions, and returns logits over what token the model thinks should come next in the sequence.

* The model uses a combination of attention and MLP blocks to process the input sequence.

* The attention block projects the current token into three vectors: a query, a key, and a value, and uses these vectors to compute attention weights and a weighted sum of the cached values.

* The MLP block is a two-layer feed-forward network that projects up to 4x the embedding dimension, applies ReLU, and projects back down.

* The model uses residual connections to add the output of each block back to its input.

* The final hidden state is projected to vocabulary size by `lm_head`, producing one logit per token in the vocabulary.

* The script is available on GitHub and can be run as a Google Colab notebook.

* The author has also created a video explaining the autograd engine and the `Value` class.

* The script has been simplified to its bare essentials, with no unnecessary dependencies or code.

* The model is trained using a simple Adam optimizer and a cross-entropy loss function.

* The script generates new, plausible-sounding names by sampling from the trained model.

* The generated names are printed to the console, along with their corresponding token IDs and positions in the sequence.

Neural Computers

Extract ALL key points from the source:

  • A paper by Meta and KAIST explores the concept of a "neural computer" that unifies computation, memory, and I/O in a learned runtime state.
  • The idea is to create a system that can act as a traditional computer, with a neural network that can learn to execute tasks.
  • The paper presents two experiments using a generative video model and curated training data to create neural computers based on a command-line interface (CLI) and a graphical user-interface (GUI).
  • The prototype works, but with limited symbolic stability and requiring more computation and data.
  • The long-term target is a Completely Neural Computer (CNC), a fully learned computer with unified compute, memory, and interfaces.
  • The authors suggest that progress toward CNCs will depend on stronger models and sustained reuse, consistency, and governance.

Recursive Self-Improvement and Economic Growth

Extract ALL key points from the source:

  • Economists from Forethought, Columbia University, and the University of Virginia model the potential economic impact of recursive self-improvement of AI systems.
  • The model identifies two channels through which automation generates explosive dynamics: technological feedback loops and economic feedback loops.
  • Key findings include:

- 13% automation across all sectors is sufficient to push the economy into explosive growth.

- 17% automation of software and hardware research is sufficient to reach the explosive growth threshold.

- Hardware research is the dominant lever, with returns to research roughly five times those in software.

- 20% automation of hardware alone is enough to cross the threshold.

- Software automation in isolation is approximately at the knife-edge, requiring a small push elsewhere to tip the system into explosive growth.

  • The authors suggest that the singularity could be closer than expected, with an "automation shock" involving full automation of software R&D and 5% automation across the rest of the economy potentially causing the singularity to arrive in roughly six years.

Details ▸

Who Restores the Peg? A Mean-Field Game Approach to Model Stablecoin Market Dynamics(https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18991)

Extract of key points:

  • The paper examines the behavior of market participants during stablecoin de-pegging events and the collective actions that restore or break the peg.
  • USDC and USDT are the dominant stablecoins pegged to $1, with a total market capitalization of over $300B.
  • The authors develop a dynamic, agent-based mean-field game framework for fiat-collateralized stablecoins.
  • The framework models the strategic interaction of a large population of arbitrageurs and retail traders across primary and secondary markets during a de-peg episode.
  • The equilibrium formulation endogenously maps market frictions into a market-clearing price path and implied net order flows.
  • The authors use three historical de-peg events to calibrate the equilibrium, which reproduces observed recovery half-lives.
  • The results yield an order flow decomposition, showing that system-wide stress is predominantly stabilized by primary-market arbitrage.
  • A quantitative sensitivity analysis identifies a non-linear breakdown threshold, beyond which a de-peg becomes markedly slower to reverse.

Modeling Dynamic Correlation Matrices with Shrinkage Priors(https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06818)

Extract of key points:

  • The paper proposes a Bayesian approach to estimate time-varying correlation matrices using a low-rank factor representation.
  • The approach includes latent states evolving under a dynamic shrinkage prior and observation errors following a multivariate factor stochastic volatility model.
  • This specification allows for locally adaptive regularization of the estimated correlation structure over time and informative uncertainty quantification.
  • A posterior contraction result is established for dynamically regularized Bayesian models, showing contraction around the true model parameters at an explicit rate under averaged Hellinger distance.
  • The paper introduces a scalar measure of cross-sectional dependence based on the information-theoretic concept of total correlation to summarize the estimated correlation matrices.
  • Simulation studies demonstrate improved accuracy and responsiveness of the proposed method relative to competing methods in various scenarios.
  • The method is applied to monitoring the correlation evolution of equity portfolios during periods of financial market stress, providing a framework for assessing the changing benefits of diversification in backtesting analyses.

Stochastic Policy Gradient Methods in the Uncertain Volatility Model(https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06670)

Extract ALL key points from the source:

  • The paper proposes a backward actor-critic stochastic policy gradient scheme for the Uncertain Volatility Model.
  • The method combines discrete dynamic programming with Proximal Policy Optimization and shallow neural-network approximations.
  • The policy parameterization uses a squashed Gaussian policy built on a C-vine representation of correlation matrices.
  • Numerical experiments show the method yields accurate prices and compares favorably with existing Monte Carlo and machine-learning-based benchmarks.

Extrema, Barrier Options, and Semi-Analytic Leverage Corrections in Stochastic-Clock Volatility Models(https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06677)

Extract ALL key points from the source:

  • The paper studies a class of continuous-path stochastic-clock volatility models with a Brownian motion run on a random increasing clock.
  • A broad family of barrier-relevant objects reduces to one-dimensional quantities determined by the Laplace transform of the terminal clock.
  • The paper develops a systematic small-ρ expansion around the ρ=0 backbone to incorporate leverage without forfeiting tractability.
  • Two implementable leverage-correction routes are proposed: forced PDEs and a Duhamel-type Monte Carlo representation.
  • Padé acceleration can extend practical accuracy to equity-like correlations.

Extract ALL key points from the source:

  • The approach uses coupled physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) to predict both the option price and the free boundary.

  • Numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed learning framework.

Forecasting implied volatility surface with generative diffusion models(https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07571)

Extract ALL key points from the source:

  • A parameter-free weighting scheme based on the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) is used to incorporate the arbitrage penalty into the loss function.
  • Numerical experiments demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed model in volatility forecasting.
